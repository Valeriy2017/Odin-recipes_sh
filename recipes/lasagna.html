Smothered Chicken Breasts

This satisfying, quick-cooking, one-skillet chicken dish combines crispy bacon, caramelized onions, and gooey shredded cheese; and clean-up is a breeze!

Ingredients

  • 4 (6 ounce) skinless, boneless chicken breast halves
  • ¼ teaspoon salt
  • ¼ teaspoon lemon pepper seasoning
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 8 strips bacon
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • ¼ cup packed brown sugar
  • ½ cup shredded Colby-Monterey Jack cheese

Directions

  1. Sprinkle chicken with salt and lemon pepper.
  2. Heat oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Cook chicken breasts until no longer pink in the center and the juices run clear, 13 to 15 minutes. An instant-read thermometer inserted into the center should read at least 165 degrees F (74 degrees C). Remove and keep warm.
  3. Place bacon in a large skillet and cook over medium-high heat, turning occasionally until evenly browned, about 10 minutes. Transfer bacon slices to a paper-towel-lined plate. Reserve 2 tablespoons drippings in the skillet. Add onion and brown sugar and cook, stirring occasionally until onion is golden, about 5 minutes.
